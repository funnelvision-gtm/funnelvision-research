# FunnelVision — Competitive Landscape

## How FunnelVision Fits

FunnelVision sits at the intersection of **CRM data hygiene** and **sales rep enablement via messaging apps** (Slack/Teams). The competitive landscape breaks into several categories, with varying degrees of overlap.

---

## Direct Competitors

### 1. Supered (https://www.supered.io/) — YOUR IDENTIFIED COMPETITOR

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What they do** | Digital adoption platform that embeds process guidance, training, and documentation directly into HubSpot and Salesforce |
| **Overlap with FunnelVision** | Helps sales teams follow CRM processes, improves data hygiene through in-app guidance |
| **Key difference** | Supered works *inside* the CRM (in-app overlays/guides). FunnelVision works *outside* the CRM (Slack/Teams). Fundamentally different philosophy. |
| **Funding** | Not publicly disclosed |
| **Customers** | 40+ testimonials; serves HubSpot partners and enterprises including Payrix, MDB Health Services, Streamline Healthcare |
| **Pricing** | Per-user, with separate company and consultant tiers |
| **HubSpot ecosystem** | Listed on HubSpot marketplace |

**Competitive take:** Supered tries to make reps better *at using HubSpot*. FunnelVision accepts that reps won't use HubSpot and meets them where they are. These are philosophically opposed approaches to the same problem — which makes Supered the most interesting competitor to position against.

---

### 2. Rattle (https://www.gorattle.com/) — CLOSEST FUNCTIONAL COMPETITOR

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What they do** | Revenue workflow engine — bi-directional Salesforce/HubSpot ↔ Slack/Teams integration for alerts, deal rooms, and CRM updates |
| **Overlap with FunnelVision** | Sends CRM notifications to Slack, lets reps update CRM from Slack, enforces data compliance via nudges |
| **Key difference** | Rattle is broader (deal rooms, workflow automation, approvals) and primarily Salesforce-focused. FunnelVision is purpose-built for HubSpot data hygiene rules. |
| **Funding** | **$28.8M** total (Sequoia Capital India, Lightspeed, Insight Partners, GV) |
| **Employees** | ~28-71 (conflicting sources; headcount reportedly declining ~30% YoY) |
| **Status** | Appears to be contracting — declining headcount is a signal worth watching |

**Competitive take:** Rattle is the most functionally similar competitor. However, its declining headcount and broad Salesforce-first focus leave a gap in the HubSpot-specific market. FunnelVision's tight focus on HubSpot deal data hygiene with rules-based enforcement is more opinionated and specific.

## Key Takeaways

### FunnelVision's Differentiation
1. **HubSpot-specific** — Most competitors are Salesforce-first or Salesforce-only. The HubSpot data hygiene space is significantly less crowded.
2. **Messaging-native delivery** — Meets reps in Slack/Teams rather than trying to make them use HubSpot better (Supered) or building another desktop app (Scratchpad).
3. **Leader-driven workflow** — Unique combination of rules + leader requests + rep responses. No competitor has this sales-leader-as-orchestrator model.
4. **Behaviour-first philosophy** — Not trying to automate the human out of the loop. Acknowledging that sales reps are humans who live in messaging apps.

### Market Signals
- **Troops acquisition by Salesforce** validates the "CRM via messaging" category
- **Dooly shutting down** shows the "yet another workspace" approach is vulnerable
- **Rattle's declining headcount** suggests the broad/Salesforce-first approach may be struggling
- **Sweep raising $50M** shows CRM governance is well-funded but aimed at admins, not reps
- **HubSpot investing in native tools** is both a threat (platform risk) and validation (the problem is real)

### Gaps to Watch
- No well-funded competitor is doing exactly what FunnelVision does (HubSpot + Slack/Teams + rules-based hygiene + leader requests)
- The Salesforce ecosystem is crowded; the HubSpot ecosystem is wide open for this use case
- Supered is the most direct philosophical competitor but takes the opposite approach (make HubSpot better vs. bypass HubSpot)
