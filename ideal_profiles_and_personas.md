# FunnelVision — Ideal Customer Profile, Ideal Partner Profile & Personas

## Part 1: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Derived from: Colossyan, CLIMATEX, Cinch

| Attribute | Pattern from Current Customers | ICP Criteria |
|-----------|-------------------------------|--------------|
| **CRM** | All use HubSpot | HubSpot Sales Hub Pro or Enterprise |
| **Company stage** | Seed to Series B | Post-funding, scaling commercial operations |
| **Team size** | 15–60 employees | 10–100 employees, with at least 5 using HubSpot deals |
| **Sales team** | Small teams managing enterprise relationships | 5–30 sales reps |
| **Industry** | AI/SaaS, deep-tech, MarTech | B2B technology companies (SaaS, AI, deep-tech) — but not limited to tech |
| **Funding** | €3.5M–$28.2M | Recently funded (within last 12–24 months) |
| **Growth phase** | All scaling from early traction to repeatable sales | Moving from founder-led sales to a structured GTM process |
| **Geography** | Hungary, Switzerland, Utah | English-speaking or English-first markets (UK, Europe, Australia, Canada, US) |
| **Pain signals** | Small teams with big-name clients, distributed teams, R&D-to-sales transitions | High CRM burden per head — too few people managing too many deals |
| **Internal comms** | Slack or Microsoft Teams as primary workspace | Already living in Slack/Teams day-to-day |

### Core ICP Statement

> FunnelVision's ideal customer is a **funded B2B company (Seed to Series B) with 5–30 sales reps using HubSpot**, where a small commercial team is managing a growing pipeline of enterprise-grade deals. They've recently raised capital and are scaling from founder-led sales into a structured GTM motion. Their reps live in Slack or Teams, not HubSpot. Their sales leaders know the data is messy but don't have the time or headcount to chase it.

### Key Buying Triggers

1. **Just raised a round** — new board expectations for pipeline visibility and forecast accuracy
2. **Hired first sales leader or VP Sales** — someone new walks in and sees the data mess
3. **Growing from 5 to 15+ reps** — the point where informal CRM discipline breaks down
4. **Lost a deal or missed a forecast due to bad data** — the acute pain moment
5. **RevOps hire or promotion** — someone whose job it now is to care about data quality
6. **HubSpot upgrade to Pro/Enterprise** — signals investment in CRM maturity

## Part 2: Customer Personas

### Persona 1: "Sarah the Sales Leader"

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Title** | VP Sales, Head of Sales, Sales Director |
| **Company** | Series A/B SaaS, 20–80 employees |
| **Reports to** | CEO or CRO |
| **Team size** | Manages 5–20 reps |
| **Age range** | 30–45 |
| **Day-to-day tools** | Slack/Teams, HubSpot (dashboards only), Gong/Clari, Google Sheets for pipeline reviews |

**Goals:**
- Hit quarterly revenue targets
- Deliver accurate forecasts to the board
- Build a repeatable, scalable sales process
- Spend time coaching reps, not chasing admin

**Frustrations:**
- "I spend Monday mornings going through every deal in HubSpot looking for what's missing before our pipeline review"
- "My forecast is only as good as the data, and the data is always 2 weeks behind"
- "I've told the team 50 times to update close dates and next steps. They just don't."
- "We had a board meeting where I couldn't explain why we missed the quarter — the data wasn't there"

**How FunnelVision helps:**
- Rules automatically flag stale deals and missing fields — she doesn't have to manually audit
- Reps get nudged in Slack to fill gaps — she doesn't have to be the nagger
- Dashboard gives her a real-time view of pipeline hygiene before every review
- Forecast-grade data without having to police it

**What she'd say to a colleague:**
> "It's like having an extra pair of eyes on the pipeline that never takes a day off. I actually trust the numbers going into our board pack now."

**Buying behaviour:**
- Champions the tool internally — she's the one with the pain
- Needs to justify spend to CEO or CFO — ROI in forecast accuracy and time saved
- Will trial quickly if setup is easy — doesn't want a 6-week implementation
- Influenced by peers (other sales leaders, LinkedIn content)

## Part 3: Ideal Partner Profile (IPP)

### Derived from: ProsperOHub/Siloy, RevCloud, b10, RevOps Works, RevOps Rocks, Flowbird

| Attribute | Pattern from Current Partners | IPP Criteria |
|-----------|-------------------------------|--------------|
| **HubSpot status** | Certified to Elite | Active HubSpot Solutions Partner (any tier) |
| **Core service** | CRM implementation, RevOps, workflow automation | Regularly implements or optimises HubSpot Sales Hub for clients |
| **Client type** | B2B scale-ups, enterprise, some public sector | Clients with 5+ sales reps using HubSpot deals |
| **Team size** | Solo fractional to 300+ employees | Any — but engagement model differs by size |
| **Geography** | UK, Europe, Australia, US | English-speaking markets (aligns with FunnelVision's rollout) |
| **Relationship depth** | Ongoing advisory, not one-off projects | Retainer or ongoing optimisation relationships with clients |
| **Pain they see in clients** | Dirty CRM data, reps not adopting processes, forecast inaccuracy | Regular exposure to "my reps won't fill in HubSpot" problem |
| **Tech sophistication** | Ranges from pure HubSpot to multi-platform + automation tools | Comfortable recommending and integrating third-party tools |

### Core IPP Statement

> FunnelVision's ideal partner is a **HubSpot Solutions Partner who regularly implements or optimises Sales Hub for B2B clients with 5+ sales reps**. They have ongoing client relationships (not just one-off implementations), and their clients frequently struggle with CRM data hygiene and rep adoption. They're looking for tools that solve real client problems, generate recurring referral revenue, and make their own advisory work more effective.

### What Makes a Partner Activate (vs just sign up)

The difference between a partner who signs and a partner who refers comes down to:

1. **They've felt the pain themselves** — they've spent hours cleaning client data or building workarounds
2. **FunnelVision is easy to demo** — they can show it to a client in a meeting without preparation
3. **It makes them look good** — recommending FunnelVision solves a problem the client already has
4. **The economics work** — referral fee is meaningful relative to their revenue model
5. **It doesn't compete with their services** — it complements their implementation/advisory work
