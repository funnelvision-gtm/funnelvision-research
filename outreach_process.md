# FunnelVision - Outreach Process & Recommendations

## Target Audience

**Primary target:** HubSpot consultants and RevOps agencies — partnership-led motion.

The go-to-market is built around partnering with firms that already work inside HubSpot with their clients, rather than selling directly to end-users.

### Partner Profile (from current network)

| Tier | Example Partners | Typical Profile |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Elite/Diamond** | ProsperOHub/Siloy, Flowbird | Large teams (50-300+), hundreds of implementations, high credibility |
| **Gold** | RevOps Rocks | Lean/fractional, high-touch client relationships, global reach |
| **Certified** | RevCloud, b10, RevOps Works | Specialist firms, startup/scale-up focus, often multi-platform |

### End-Customer ICP (via partners)
Based on current customers (Colossyan, CLIMATEX, Cinch):
- **Funded SaaS/tech companies** — Series A to growth stage
- **Small-to-mid teams** (15-60 people) managing enterprise relationships
- **High CRM burden per head** — small teams with big-name clients
- **Scaling rapidly** — the stage where CRM discipline either holds or breaks

## Objection Analysis (with competitive context)

### "We already do this in HubSpot"

This is the most important objection to crack. HubSpot's native Data Quality Command Center offers duplicate detection, required fields, and property validation — but it has clear gaps:

- **No Slack/Teams nudging** — native tools enforce at point of entry, requiring reps to actually be in HubSpot
- **No leader-driven request workflow** — there's no way for a sales leader to identify a gap and trigger a specific request to a specific rep
- **Limited rules engine** — basic property validation, not the flexible hygiene rules FunnelVision offers

**The real issue:** This objection often means the prospect hasn't grasped the behavioural insight — that reps *don't* fill things in at point of entry, and no amount of required fields fixes that. The message needs to lead with the behaviour problem, not the feature.

### "Not enough value / just Slack actioning"

This suggests the messaging is landing as "a Slack bot that pings people" rather than "a system that gives sales leaders control over data quality." Competitors who've succeeded in this space frame value around:
- **Relief from chasing** — the #1 customer sentiment across Rattle, Momentum, and Supered buyers
- **Force multiplication** — "does the work of 2-3 people" (resonates with lean RevOps teams)
- **Forecast accuracy** — clean data = reliable forecasts, which is a board-level concern

The current Message 1 mentions "forecast-grade data" but buries it. It should be more prominent.

### "Needs more functionality"

Cross-object support and reporting requests signal that prospects see potential but want FunnelVision to be more than point-solution. This is actually a buying signal — they're telling you what it would take.

### "I've built this with Claude Code"

This is a fascinating objection unique to the current moment. It signals:
- The prospect recognises the problem exists
- They've tried to solve it themselves (validating the need)
- They think a quick hack is sufficient (may not have hit scale/maintenance pain yet)

Homegrown solutions break when: the team grows, rules get complex, multiple people need to manage it, or the person who built it leaves.

## Recommendations

### 1. Reframe the opening message around the behaviour problem, not the product

The current Message 1 leads with what FunnelVision does. Competitor messaging analysis shows that the most effective approaches lead with the pain. Supered's "litany of futility" approach and Rattle's metric-driven headlines both outperform feature descriptions.

**Current framing:** "I'm building FunnelVision to remove admin associated with pipeline hygiene"
**Problem:** This is founder-centric ("I'm building") and feature-centric ("remove admin"). It doesn't speak to what the *partner* cares about — their clients' pipeline accuracy and the RevOps team's sanity.

**Suggested reframe for Message 1:**
> Hi [NAME],
>
> Quick question — how many of your HubSpot clients have reps who just don't fill in deal data?
>
> We've built FunnelVision so sales leaders can set hygiene rules on their pipeline and reps get nudged to fix gaps directly in Slack or Teams — no HubSpot login needed.
>
> We're partnering with HubSpot agencies and offering free onboarding. Worth a quick look?

**Why this is better:**
- Opens with a question the partner will recognise from every client engagement
- "No HubSpot login needed" is the sharpest differentiator vs native tools and Supered
- Positions from the partner's perspective (their clients' problem) not the founder's perspective

### 2. Make Message 2 work harder

The current follow-up is generic. It should introduce new information or social proof.

**Suggested reframe for Message 2:**
> Following up — we're already working with partners like [TIER-APPROPRIATE EXAMPLE] and early customers include AI companies managing enterprise accounts.
>
> Happy to set you up with free partner access so you can see it with your own clients. 15 mins next week?

**Why:** Namedropping partners (ProsperOHub/Siloy as Elite, Flowbird as Diamond) creates credibility. Mentioning "AI companies managing enterprise accounts" (i.e. Colossyan) signals the ICP without needing to name them.

### 3. Add a value-specific Message 3 before the check-in

Consider a 4-message sequence where Message 3 tackles the most common objection head-on, and the current Message 3 becomes Message 4.

**Suggested new Message 3:**
> I know some teams feel HubSpot's native tools cover this — the difference is FunnelVision puts the request in Slack where reps actually respond, and gives leaders a dashboard to see what's missing across the whole pipeline.
>
> Flat £5K/year regardless of team size, so it works for clients scaling from 10 to 50 reps without the cost scaling too.

**Why:** Pre-empts the #1 objection, introduces the pricing advantage (which is compelling for partners advising growing teams), and highlights the leader dashboard which no competitor offers.

### 4. Build objection-specific responses

For prospects who respond with objections rather than silence:

**"We do this already in HubSpot"**
> Totally fair — HubSpot's native tools are solid for required fields at point of entry. Where clients usually find the gap is the data that goes stale *after* the deal is created — stages that sit too long, fields that were optional but become critical for forecasting. That's where the Slack nudge changes things. Happy to show the difference if useful?

**"I've built this with Claude Code"**
> Love that — shows the problem's real! What we hear is that homegrown solutions work great for one person but get hard to maintain as the team grows or rules change. FunnelVision gives leaders a no-code dashboard to manage rules themselves. Might be worth comparing?

**"Needs more functionality"**
> Really appreciate that feedback — can I ask what specifically you'd need? We're actively building and partner input directly shapes the roadmap.

### 5. Tailor messaging by partner tier

Not all partners are the same. The pitch to an Elite partner managing enterprise deployments is different from a fractional RevOps consultant.

**For Elite/Diamond partners (ProsperOHub, Flowbird):**
- Lead with scale: "Your clients are growing sales teams — FunnelVision keeps hygiene clean without per-seat cost scaling"
- Emphasise white-glove: "We handle onboarding so it's zero lift for your team"
- Referral economics matter: at £5K/year flat, discuss rev-share openly

**For boutique/fractional (RevOps Rocks, RevOps Works):**
- Lead with the tool-in-your-stack angle: "Another tool you can deploy for every client"
- Emphasise ease: "Takes 15 minutes to set up rules, works immediately"
- These partners are hands-on — they'll want to try it themselves first

**For multi-platform partners (b10):**
- Lead with HubSpot differentiation: "Nothing like this exists for HubSpot — it's all been Salesforce until now"
- Their clients may be comparing CRMs — FunnelVision makes HubSpot more viable for larger teams

### 6. Leverage the competitive gap in messaging

The competitive landscape reveals a significant positioning opportunity that isn't being used:

- **Rattle** (closest competitor) is Salesforce-first and declining in headcount
- **Troops** was acquired by Salesforce (validating the category)
- **Dooly** shut down
- **No well-funded competitor** does HubSpot + Slack/Teams + rules-based hygiene + leader requests

This means FunnelVision can honestly say: "There's nothing else like this for HubSpot." That's a powerful line for partner conversations — partners want to recommend tools where they're offering something unique.

### 7. Use competitor customer language in your own messaging

The most resonant customer sentiment across the competitive landscape is **relief from chasing/nagging**. This should be the emotional core of FunnelVision's pitch:

| Resonance Rank | Theme | How to use it |
|---------------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | **Relief** — "I don't have to chase people anymore" | Lead with this in partner conversations — their clients' RevOps teams are drowning in follow-ups |
| 2 | **Habit** — "It became part of how we work" | Use in case study language once Colossyan has been using it longer |
| 3 | **Leverage** — "Force multiplier for small teams" | Maps perfectly to ICP (small teams, enterprise relationships) |
| 4 | **Metrics** — Time saved, accuracy up | Build these from Colossyan/CLIMATEX/Cinch usage data when available |

### 8. Pricing as a selling point to partners

The flat £5,000/year model is a genuine competitive advantage when selling through partners — but it's not in any of the current messaging. Partners advise clients on tool costs. Being able to say "it's £5K flat, whether you have 5 reps or 50" is a conversation-stopper in a good way.

At 10+ reps, FunnelVision is cheaper than every per-user competitor. At 20+ reps it's dramatically cheaper. Partners whose clients are scaling will recognise this immediately.

**Consider including in partner pitch:** "No per-seat pricing means your clients' costs don't scale as they grow — makes your recommendation easier to defend at budget time."
