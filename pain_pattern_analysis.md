## Pain Theme 1: CRM Data Entry is Painful, Slow, and Time-Consuming

**Mentions: ~85 reviews (Scratchpad: ~70, Rattle: ~8, Momentum: ~5, Supered: ~2)**
**Most mentioned by:** Scratchpad reviewers (overwhelmingly)
**Primary persona:** Marcus the RevOps Manager, Sarah the Sales Leader
**FunnelVision relevance: Medium**

This is the single most cited pain across all reviews. Reps hate the CRM interface and avoid it. The pain is about friction — too many clicks, slow page loads, clunky UI, and time wasted navigating between records.

### Best Verbatim Quotes

1. **Jeff Hanks | Director | Pluralsight (Scratchpad):** "After 8-10 hours a day in Salesforce working on notation and everything else I have to do, the thing I dread the most is updating everything in Salesforce one by one. Scratchpad literally saves me 60-90 minutes a day."

2. **Erik Long | Account Executive | Front (Scratchpad):** "Before I would spend at least an hour before our weekly pipeline meetings to make updates on deals. This would mean opening up almost every opp to update next steps, dates, notes, etc."

3. **Cameron Vens | Sales Manager | Aetion (Scratchpad):** "Admin is always a nightmare and this allows us to take care of it in minutes versus hours."

4. **Mackenzie Clements | Customer Success Ops Manager | Crunchbase (Scratchpad):** "No one likes updating things in Salesforce, it's clunky, it's not easy to find all required fields, reps want spreadsheet like views."

5. **Jose Almeida | EMEA Account Executive | Talkdesk (Scratchpad):** "The reality is that all sales people should use Scratchpad. The easiness of use and friendly UI now guarantees that I do not spend 30 minutes after a call updating my CRM."

### FunnelVision Implication
FunnelVision does not solve this pain directly. FunnelVision is not a CRM interface replacement — it does not make data entry faster. However, it addresses the *downstream consequence* of this pain: when reps avoid CRM data entry, FunnelVision catches the gaps via Slack nudges. FunnelVision is complementary to tools like Scratchpad rather than competitive on this pain.
